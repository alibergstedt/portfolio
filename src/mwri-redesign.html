Approach

Defining the goals and strategy

When I approach a project, especially one as large as a complete ecommerce website redesign, research and questions are important. I first like to meet with clients, ask questions, do market research, analyze the competitors, and get user feedback. This is vital in defining what our ultimate goals will be and what problems the design will be solving.

For MWRI, the issues were not in the product itself. The customers raved about the product and service of the company seen through their many glowing reviews. The complaints from the company were customer retention (rarely did they see customers coming back for more than one order) and an inconsistent branding experience. After doing additional market analysis and looking at website traffic analytics, I was able to determine some additional painpoints that needed solving including the length of their forms and call to action buttons.

The main goals I set out to solve was to create a more cohesive, trustworthy design to solve the problem with credibility. I also set out to determine how to increase customer retention.

Results

The result

After meetings and discussions with the client, to solve to issue with customer retention, a new website name was proposed. My Wedding Reception Ideas was too long and was created in the days of organic SEO instead of created with memorability in mind. However, when rebranding a company, a new company name is a decision that should not be taken lightly. It can affect your search engine rankings and involves a signficant amount of marketing and SEO work. While I felt this was the best way forward for memorability and longevitiy of the site brand, MWRI decided that was a change they were not ready to make.

Instead we produced a cohesive modern site through color, typography, and iconography that represented a credible and welcoming wedding website. We chose a color scheme that was both muted and soft yet bold in our call to action colors. For our display fonts, we chose a romantic serif font. Both catered to our gender and age demographic.

Additionally I did a comprehensive analysis of the site and determined how many different button colors, font styles, and variations existed in the design. We worked to ensure the new design stayed under a total of three font styles and stuck to a consistently branded color scheme and theme. This was imperative for creating a more trustworthy and credible website experience.

Another change that was made was to the product pages. This page was very personalization with many different form fields. We simplified the screen by enlarging the product image, removing unecessary buttons, bringing consistency through color and typogrphy, and bringing focus to the ultimate goal of conversion.
